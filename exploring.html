Exploring Death Valley

A few favorites near the hotel—quick, iconic, and worth the stop.

Part of our draw to Death Valley is the unexpected beauty. Yes, it’s famous for being the hottest place on Earth, but it’s also full of dramatic landscapes and awe-inspiring views. We hope you carve out a little time to explore— even a quick drive can feel like you’ve landed on another planet. Below are a few highlights near the hotel (and there’s plenty more on the National Park website).

Pro tip
Cell service is limited. Download offline maps before you head out.

Zabriskie Point

Classic sunrise/sunset views with minimal walking.

  • ~10–15 min from The Inn
  • Vault toilets available
  • Great for photos

Artist’s Palette

A short scenic drive with colorful hills (and one of our favorites).

  • ~25–30 min from The Inn
  • Easy from Zabriskie Point
  • Vault toilets available

Badwater Basin

The lowest point in North America—wide open and surreal.

  • ~20–25 min from The Inn
  • Flat, easy walking
  • Bring sunglasses + water

Mesquite Flat Sand Dunes

Short walk from the parking lot to dunes—great near golden hour.

  • ~20–25 min from The Inn
  • Sand + wind = fun hair
  • Closed-toe shoes recommended

Short “Do This If You Only Have 2 Hours” Plan

  1. Drive to Zabriskie Point for the view
  2. Continue to Artist’s Palette for the colors
  3. Head back to the hotel for a drink at The Oasis
Low effort, high reward.
