Dress Code & Weather

Desert wedding weekend — comfortable, elevated, and photo-friendly.

Dress Code

Wedding attire: Cocktail / Semi-formal

  • Think: polished, fun, and something you feel good in
  • Comfortable shoes encouraged (rocky ground at the ceremony)
  • Bring a layer — desert temps can shift quickly
Color vibe (optional):
Desert tones, neutrals, warm earth colors, and soft jewel tones photograph beautifully here.

Weather

Death Valley is famous for heat… but in January it can be wonderfully mild.

  • Days: often pleasant and sunny
  • Evenings: can get chilly (especially with wind)
  • Sun is still strong — sunglasses and SPF are your friends

We’ll share a more specific forecast closer to the weekend.

What to Pack

  • Wedding outfit + backup layer
  • Comfortable shoes (ceremony + exploring)
  • Light jacket / shawl / blazer
  • Sunglasses
  • SPF / chapstick
  • Water bottle
  • Optional: hat
  • Optional: small flashlight/headlamp (if you’re out stargazing)
  • Phone charger / portable battery
  • Downloaded offline maps

What Not to Worry About

  • Perfection. We’re in the desert. A little wind, a little dust, and a little adventure are part of the charm.
  • Being “too dressed up” or “not dressed up enough.” If you’re comfortable and feel good, you nailed it.
  • Hair and makeup surviving the elements. You’ll still look amazing — and the photos will too.
  • Knowing the schedule by heart. Just show up for the lobby meet and caravan. We’ll handle the rest.
Bottom line
Come comfortable, bring a layer, and plan to have fun. That’s the whole dress code.
